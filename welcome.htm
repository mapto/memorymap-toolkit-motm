Minors on the Move: Mapping forced migration from Nazism and its testimonies

This website is part of the Minors on the Move project which explores the experiences of Jewish children and teenagers who were forced to leave Germany and Nazi-occupied territories during the 1930s and 1940s. At its heart are two oral history collections held at the Archive for Spoken German (AGD) of the Leibniz Institute for the German Language in Mannheim:

  • The Israelkorpus, comprising testimonies of people who emigrated to Palestine/Israel — including children who travelled unaccompanied through the Jugendalijah programme — and their children;
  • The Flucht und Emigration nach Großbritannien (FEGB) corpus, with interviews from Kindertransport children and young migrants of the same period.

To explore these questions, this website lets you navigate the testimonies through following the individual journeys.

Funded by the Italian Ministry of University and Research (PRIN 2022, Prot. 2022FSHZET). Project duration: 24 months, February 2025 – February 2027.
